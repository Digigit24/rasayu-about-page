Clinical outcomes of Ayurvedic medicines used in treating chronic liver disease: A case series

Yogesh Bendale, Ketaki Jagtap, Avinash Kadam, Poonam Birari-Gawande
Correspondence – Yogesh Bendale – rasyaurpublication2020@gmail.com
Rasayu Ayurved Clinic, Flat No.1 and 2, Shreevijaya Apts, Pune 411 052, India

Recent developments in the understanding of the natural history and pathophysiology of liver cirrhosis and the management of its complications have improved patient care, quality of life, and life expectancy. However, for a limited number of patients, liver transplantation is the only curative option. Even today, liver surgery remains one of the most technically challenging surgical procedures in abdominal surgery. The exorbitant cost involved in this procedure also is a factor for many patients to avoid surgery.

There are few evidences which highlight that Complementary and Alternative Medicine (CAM) is frequently used by chronic liver disease (CLD) patients to treat CLD-specific health issues. In India, Ayurvedic is one of the main complementary therapies used by patients with CLD. However, there is paucity of evidence about its efficacy in management of patients with CLD.

Here, we are reporting three cases of male patients with liver cirrhosis. These patients reported to our outpatient department with a radiologically confirmed diagnosis of liver cirrhosis. They were treated for 6, 9 and 9 months respectively with exclusive Ayurveda therapy consisting of orally administered Herbo-mineral Ayurveda formulations. Their total bilirubin at baseline vs. end of therapy were 3.15 vs. 0.53, 7.7 vs. 0.9, 2.1 vs. 0.9 respectively.

These patients were having gross, moderate and mild ascites respectively at baseline, which resolved completely after the treatment.

The present case reports demonstrate the potential of Ayurveda in improving the therapeutic outcomes in patients of liver cirrhosis. Ayurveda therapy can be considered as a therapeutic option for patients with liver cirrhosis who are not eligible or not willing to opt for surgical approach in liver cirrhosis management. However, further studies are required to generate high level of evidence.

"The resolution of ascites and significant bilirubin reduction in our patients highlights the transformative potential of standardized Ayurvedic compounds."
